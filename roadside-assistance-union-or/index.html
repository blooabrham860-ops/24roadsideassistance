Frequently Asked Questions

1. What services are included in basic roadside assistance?

It usually covers tire changes, battery jumps, lockouts, and fuel delivery. Some plans add towing up to a certain distance.

2. How soon can roadside help arrive?

In cities, expect 15-30 minutes. In remote areas, it might take 45-60 minutes. We send updates via app or text.

3. Does roadside assistance help with flat tires?

Yes, we can repair or replace the spare if you have one. For bigger damage, we arrange a tow to a shop.

4. Is there roadside coverage for motorcycles or RVs?

Standard plans focus on cars, but options like roadside service are made for RVs and bikes with special tools.

Union, OR Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Union is a city in Union County, Oregon, United States, originally platted in 1864, and located 15 miles (24 km) southeast of La Grande. It is the namesake of Union County, which references the Union states, or Northern States, of the American Civil War. The population was 2,152 at the 2020 census. The city is known for the numerous historic Victorian homes that line its Main Street, some of which are registered on the National Register of Historic Places. It is also home to Oregon State University's Eastern Oregon Agricultural Research Center, founded in 1888, which is contemporarily housed in the former Union train station.
