Frequently Asked Questions

1. What services are included in basic roadside assistance?

It usually covers tire changes, battery jumps, lockouts, and fuel delivery. Some plans add towing up to a certain distance.

2. How soon can roadside help arrive?

In cities, expect 15-30 minutes. In remote areas, it might take 45-60 minutes. We send updates via app or text.

3. Does roadside assistance help with flat tires?

Yes, we can repair or replace the spare if you have one. For bigger damage, we arrange a tow to a shop.

4. Is there roadside coverage for motorcycles or RVs?

Standard plans focus on cars, but options like roadside service are made for RVs and bikes with special tools.

Racine, WI Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Racine (/rəˈsiːn, reɪ-/ rə-SEEN, ray-) is a city in and the county seat of Racine County, Wisconsin, United States. It is located on the shore of Lake Michigan at the mouth of the Root River. Racine is situated 22 miles (35 km) south of Milwaukee and approximately 60 miles (100 km) north of Chicago. It is the principal city of the US Census Bureau's Racine metropolitan area (consisting only of Racine County). The Racine metropolitan area is, in turn, counted as part of the Milwaukee combined statistical area. As of the 2020 U.S. Census, the city had a population of 77,816, making it the 5th largest city in Wisconsin. In January 2017, it was rated "the most affordable place to live in the world" by the Demographia International Housing Affordability survey.
